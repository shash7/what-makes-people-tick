The idea is to compare weather metrics of San Fransisco with the number of posts on reddit/r/SFr4r

What

This chart shows data collected from reddit personals in the San fransisco area and weather data from the corresponding days the personals were posted on.

Why

The idea is to compare two completely different datasets and see if there are any patterns that match. In this case, the point to prove is to see if bad weather leads to people posting more personals(or not).

The fact that people post less when the weather's good is still inconclusive.

Conclusion

There seems to be some correlation but nothing too significant to make a judgement.

It would be better if the dataset is bigger, spanning over a year or even a couple of years. In that way the seasons are involved too and we can figure out if they play a role in making people get out of their homes.

With the limited dataset we have, we are at a point where we can only cross it off as a case of correlation != causation.

In this case the only thing we proved is that San Fran's weather is shit.
